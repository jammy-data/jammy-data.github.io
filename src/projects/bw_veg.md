## Overview

In Catalunya, beaches are disappearing (like most places in the world... see [Vousdoukas et al., 2020](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41558-020-0697-0)). Erosion, driven by increasingly intense winter storms, continues to deplete the sandy coastlines along the Catalan coast. In places like Calafell and Montgat, the difference is substantial - what was previously a large stretch of beach has become only a few meters wide, leaving little for locals and tourists to enjoy. 

At present, many beaches are re-nourished only to have this sand washed away within the year, revealing an expensive and seemingly futile task. To that end, some municipalities have decided to let nature take its course, removing beach infrasturcture, such as toilets and cabins, further back (also called **managed retreat**). However, for some municipalities, such as Sitges, the promenade is part of the local identity and retreat is not an option.

This causes a spectrum of strategic decision making for coastal managers. Here, I aim to understand the patterns of erosion over time, drawing on open data available for Catalunya, and experimenting with ML and change analysis methods to build insights into beach dynamics over time. Part of this exploration will involve land classification.

In an ideal world, I'd extract LiDAR from coastal locations and identify intertidal morphology at the centimeter scale. A ver.
