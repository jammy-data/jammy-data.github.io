## Overview

A project that has been stuck at the bottom of a to-do list for some time. In my echo chamber, the world of AI is moving rapidly and, at times, it feels like a marathon to keep up to date. Geographic foundation models are something I've wanted to work with for some time, but don't have a thorough understanding of at the present time. I'm also following the seemingly ever-increasing capacity of LLMs to solve complex problems. With that being said, I wanted to kill 2 proverbial birds with one stone and offload this project to Claude, whilst keeping a skeptical, watchful eye on the outputs, and learning about GFMs and their potential in a use-case I have pre existing domain expertise in.

I have asked Claude to document the process of this task in computational notebooks, starting basic and narrative-heavy, before developing the ideas we generate into more complex and technical-heavy code. I want people to be able to follow along as I have, and I want to be open about my experience of supervising Claude to complete this task. What you will see in this project is ultimately the result of many _many_ back-and-forths of troubleshooting technical, ideological and philosophical problems during development.

Anyway, I learned a lot - and I hope that you can too.
